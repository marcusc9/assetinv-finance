Market scan

Demand is resilient, but the route to market is changing.

Asset finance remains one of the largest SME investment channels. Invoice finance and asset-based lending are more fragmented, service-led, and exposed to a bank retreat that creates room for trusted challengers and values-led brands.

Why it matters

Good businesses need finance that backs useful growth.

Asset finance and invoice discounting sit close to the real trading needs of SMEs: equipment, vehicles, stock, payroll, and customer payments. Used well, they help sound businesses invest and manage cash without turning every funding conversation into a general-purpose loan.

Asset finance

Backs productive investment

The Bank can support customers buying assets that make the business stronger: machinery, plant, vans, technology, energy efficiency upgrades, and other kit tied to trading activity. That fits better with an ethical strategy than unsecured lending with no clear link to purpose or outcomes.

Invoice discounting

Protects cashflow without changing the business

Many good businesses do not fail because the order book is weak; they get squeezed waiting to be paid. Confidential invoice discounting gives them working capital against invoices they have already earned, while keeping customer relationships intact.

Ethical fit

Turns values into lending choices

A partner-led model lets the Bank set clear exclusions, prioritise sectors with social or environmental value, and keep conduct standards visible. The point is not to chase volume at any price; it is to help good businesses grow on terms the Bank can stand behind.

Where value sits

Three plays are worth exploring.

  • 1

    Invoice discounting as the fastest trust extension

    SMEs already using business current accounts need cashflow support, not a lecture on capital structure. Confidential invoice discounting and selective factoring can meet that need with a partner balance sheet and specialist operations.

  • 2

    Asset finance through targeted sectors

    Start with plant, machinery, equipment, technology, and green transition assets. Avoid undifferentiated motor-led exposure while the sector is still managing conduct and commission scrutiny.

  • 3

    White-label where the Bank owns the relationship

    White-label or co-branded journeys preserve customer relevance while leaving underwriting workflow, verification, collections, and platform maintenance with a specialist.

Competitor analysis

The market gap is a service gap, not just a lending gap.

The Bank should position around trusted guidance, transparent eligibility, and ethical sectors. That gives senior stakeholders a distinctive angle against both high-street retrenchment and specialist lender complexity.

Competitor set Current posture Implication for The Co-operative Bank
Large high-street banks Selective appetite, tighter eligibility, and closures or restrictions in parts of invoice finance. Offer a guided specialist route for customers who are too small, complex, or time-poor for corporate banking channels.
Specialist banks Broad products, strong sector knowledge, but often less values-led differentiation. Partner where capability is proven; differentiate with ethical filters, relationship management, and customer advocacy.
Independent providers Operational depth, broker networks, and appetite for SME cases that mainstream banks may not prioritise. Best initial route for invoice finance pilots, with strong service-level and brand governance in the contract.
Fintech and embedded finance Fast digital journeys, API integrations, and checkout or platform-led working capital offers. Use as a second workstream to test digital origination and future white-label journeys inside business banking.

Stakeholder questions

  • A

    What role should the Bank own?

    Introducer, co-brand distributor, white-label owner, or balance-sheet lender.

  • B

    Where is ethical advantage real?

    Green assets, social enterprises, local employers, co-operatives, and productive investment.

  • C

    What must be controlled centrally?

    Customer promises, conduct, data sharing, complaints, affordability, exclusions, and vulnerability support.

Recommendation

Run a staged partner selection, starting with invoice finance.

Recommend opening structured conversations with Bibby Financial Services, Kriya / Allica, and Close Brothers. Select one invoice finance pilot partner and one asset finance benchmark partner. The goal is not to launch every product; it is to prove customer demand, operational control, and brand permission before committing capital or systems.

  • 0-90 days: partner RFI, target customer segments, conduct review, data-sharing model, and ethical exclusions.
  • 3-6 months: referral pilot for invoice discounting and factoring with SME relationship managers.
  • 6-12 months: co-branded or white-label journey if NPS, conversion, arrears, and complaint metrics pass gates.
  • 12+ months: sector-specific asset finance pilot for productive and transition-aligned assets.
